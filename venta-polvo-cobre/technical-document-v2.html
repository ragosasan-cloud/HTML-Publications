02 · Separation Process

No Plasma. No Eight-Figure Investment.

A common mistake in this market is assuming that converting copper powder into high-spec fractions requires plasma technology or capex above $10M USD. For the two strategic bands identified, the required technology is mature, commercially available, and can be installed by an operator or distributor with moderate financial strength.

Process-Critical Qualification Parameters — Chinese manufacturers of high-spec conductive pastes qualify copper-powder suppliers on the following parameters:

  • D10, D50, D90 particle-size distribution — laser diffraction (Malvern or equivalent)
  • Morphology and sphericity — SEM with quantitative image analysis
  • Surface oxide content — XPS or TPR, critical to paste conductivity
  • Apparent density and tap density — Hall flowmeter
  • Flowability and angle of repose — relevant for high-speed screen printing
  • Thermal stability — TGA, for low-temperature sintering pastes
  • Lot-to-lot consistency — coefficient of variation <5% in D50 across consecutive lots
Technical note — why consistency beats purity: In the MLCC and power-electronics paste market, a supplier at 99.2% purity with <3% D50 variation is often preferred over 99.7% purity with 12% variation. Paste formulations are tuned to a specific distribution — variation forces costly line re-optimization.

04 · Technical Conclusion

The Limiting Factor Is Not Technical

The available material — high-purity copper powder with a dendritic morphology produced under a proprietary, proven-consistency process — meets the fundamental technical requirements to access China's high-spec conductive-paste market.

The two identified separation bands cover the three highest-growth, highest-price subsegments in China: HJT solar, MLCC, and EV power electronics. Band A is a small, premium fraction of the full lot — its value is real, and its scarcity is part of what makes it premium.

The true competitive asset is the repeatability of the full distribution — something no amount of capital alone can manufacture. A buyer who qualifies to this supply relationship is qualifying to something the local market cannot consistently replicate.

The required separation technology does not require plasma or heavy-industry capex. A distributor with moderate financial capacity and controlled-atmosphere infrastructure can install full separation capability with an investment of $300,000 to $700,000 USD.

The limiting factor is not technical. It is access. And access is determined by the quality of the human link — whether that is the Connector who knows the room, or the Builder who constructs it.
