01 · The Opportunity

Why Now,
Why China

There is a real — though narrow — window of time in which a properly connected actor in China's advanced materials ecosystem can position themselves as the bridge between a high-spec copper powder producer with a proprietary process, and a buyer network in China that is currently paying $30 to $100 per gram for precisely this kind of material.

That window exists because three trends are converging at once:

  • China's solar industry transition to HJT (Heterojunction) is creating urgent demand for low-temperature conductive pastes — requiring high-purity copper powder with tightly controlled particle-size distribution.
  • China's EV value chain — CATL, BYD, and their tier-1 suppliers — is actively seeking alternatives to silver in power electronics. High-purity nano copper is the natural candidate.
  • The MLCC market is moving toward smaller geometries that require powders with more precise particle-size distributions — exactly where this material differentiates.
The Chinese Market Pain Point

China has copper powder production capacity, but it lacks batch-to-batch consistency at the specifications demanded by premium conductive-paste manufacturers. Local suppliers offer volume and price; they do not offer the combination of purity + controlled morphology + statistical consistency that the premium segment requires. That gap is the opportunity.

At this stage, we are not trying to close a sale or publish a process. We are identifying the person who can take us to the operator in China who can perform one of these roles: a strategic broker with real technical access, or an owner/operator who can build and run local separation and commercialization.

03 · The Role

The Strategic Broker —
What We Are Looking For

The broker we want is not a sales agent. This is a player with real influence inside China's advanced materials ecosystem — someone whose call is picked up immediately by materials engineers or R&D directors at the target buyers.

Ideal Broker Profile
  • Direct, active relationships with technical teams (not only commercial teams) in at least two of the following: conductive paste manufacturers, MLCC manufacturers, EV tier-1 suppliers, or solar HJT R&D labs.
  • Technical fluency in materials — no need to be a metallurgist, but able to speak the language and navigate technical conversations without translation.
  • Verifiable track record of enabling a new supplier or material entry into an established supply chain in China.
  • Physical presence or local teams in at least two of the following cities: Shenzhen, Shanghai, Suzhou, Xi'an.
  • Ability to operate discreetly — the initial conversations must remain confidential until the Trojan Horse distributor is identified and the alliance structure is defined.
What the Broker Does Not Need to Be
  • No capital required to invest in equipment — that is the distributor's responsibility.
  • No logistics infrastructure required — that is the distributor's responsibility.
  • No need to know the production process in detail — that comes from the producer's technical team.
  • No need to manage day-to-day buyer operations — that is handled by the distributor once activated.
In One Sentence

The strategic broker does something money cannot directly buy: they introduce the right material to the right person at the right time, with enough credibility that the person takes it seriously.

The Offer to the Distributor — Five Non-Negotiables
  • Certified, ready material — the producer guarantees quality, traceability, and consistency. The distributor does not carry input-quality risk.
  • Territorial exclusivity — China market protection for a defined period in exchange for volume and penetration commitments.
  • Full technical support — team training, buyer-ready documentation, and support during qualification.
  • Differentiated internal band pricing — internal transfer pricing based on real market value, not commodity cost. The distributor captures real margin from the separation process they install.
  • Joint growth roadmap — band separation is step one. Surface coatings, passivation, and functionalization are next, with the distributor as preferred partner.
