Process-Critical Parameters for Qualification

Chinese manufacturers of high-spec conductive pastes qualify copper-powder suppliers on the following parameters — each must be supported with documented methodology and real results:

  • D10, D50, D90 particle-size distribution — laser diffraction; Malvern method or equivalent
  • Morphology and sphericity — SEM with quantitative image analysis
  • Surface oxide content — XPS or TPR, critical to final paste conductivity
  • Apparent density and tap density — Hall flowmeter, impacts viscosity formulation
  • Flowability and angle of repose — relevant for high-speed screen printing
  • Thermal stability — TGA, for pastes requiring low-temperature sintering
  • Lot-to-lot consistency — coefficient of variation < 5% in D50 across consecutive lots
Technical Note · Why Consistency Beats Purity

In the MLCC and power-electronics paste market, a supplier at 99.2% purity with <3% D50 variation is often preferred over 99.7% purity with 12% variation. Paste formulations are tuned to a specific distribution — variation forces costly line re-optimization.
